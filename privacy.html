Stellune

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 May 2026

Stellune is a sky almanac that runs entirely on your device. This policy describes what data the app handles and how it is treated.

What we collect

Nothing leaves your device by default. Stellune has no user accounts, no analytics SDKs, no third-party trackers, and no advertising identifiers. We do not collect, store, or transmit any personally identifiable information about you.

Location data

If you grant location permission, Stellune uses your latitude and longitude to compute accurate sunrise, sunset, twilight, and moonrise times. Your location:

  • stays on your device,
  • is never transmitted to our servers (we do not operate any),
  • is shared only with the Apple Weather / Open-Meteo cloud-cover API if and only if you view the optional stargazing forecast card. The request includes only your latitude/longitude rounded to two decimal places — no identifier is sent.

If you decline location permission, Stellune falls back to a default city you can change in Settings. The almanac still works.

Notifications

Notifications are scheduled on-device using UNUserNotificationCenter. We do not operate a push notification server. You can disable any or all notification categories from Settings.

Cloud cover forecast (Open-Meteo)

The optional stargazing forecast feature queries api.open-meteo.com, a free weather API operated by a third party. Open-Meteo states that they do not log queries with personally identifiable information. See Open-Meteo's terms.

App Store Connect

If you download Stellune from the App Store, Apple may collect download and crash data per the Apple Privacy Policy. We see only aggregate, anonymized data through App Store Connect — never individual user information.

Children's privacy

Stellune is rated 4+ and is suitable for all ages. We do not knowingly collect any data from children, because we do not collect any data from anyone.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent change. Material changes will be noted in the app's release notes.
