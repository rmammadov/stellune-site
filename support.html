FAQ

Why is the moon phase different from another app?

Different apps use different reference epochs and rounding. Stellune computes the phase from the synodic month (29.530588 days) anchored to the 6 January 2000 new moon. On any given day the major apps usually agree to within a percent or two of illumination.

Why are the sunrise/sunset times slightly off?

Stellune's sun calculations are accurate to roughly one minute, using the standard "official" sunrise altitude (-0.833° to account for atmospheric refraction and the solar disc). If you see a difference of more than a minute or two from a trusted source, please email us with your location.

Where do you get the cloud cover forecast?

The optional stargazing forecast queries Open-Meteo, a free, open-source weather API. We don't operate any servers ourselves.

Where does the ISS pass data come from?

Stellune ships with a recent ISS Two-Line Element set (TLE) from NORAD via celestrak.org and propagates it locally using a simplified SGP4 model. We refresh the bundled TLE at each app release. If passes look stale, check the App Store for an update.

Can I turn off the astrology features?

Stellune is a sky almanac first. The "Reading" tab with horoscope, sign profiles, and compatibility is entirely optional — you can ignore it forever and the app works fully as an astronomical reference.

Does the watch app work without the iPhone?

Yes. The watch app is fully standalone — it computes its own sunrise/sunset and moon phase. It reads location from a value the iPhone has saved, and falls back to a default city if it can't see one.
